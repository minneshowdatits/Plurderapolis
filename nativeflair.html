About Native Flair

Native Flair is the creative passion of one talented Native American artist based in Minnesota. As a one-woman operation, she pours her heart and soul into transforming ordinary objects into extraordinary works of art.

Specializing in upcycling trinkets and jewelry, she breathes new life into forgotten pieces while honoring traditional Native American artistry. Her creative talents extend beyond vases to include painting, costume design, jewelry making, and more.

Every piece tells a story, with colors and patterns that reflect nature's beauty and the artist's unique perspective. The intricate designs are carefully painted on the inside of each vase, creating a delightful surprise when viewed from different angles.

Tribal Inspiration

The Saginaw Chippewa Indian Tribe of Michigan has a rich cultural heritage that inspires the Native Flair collection. Based in the Great Lakes region, the tribe's traditional artistry, beadwork, and connection to nature influence many of the designs.

Traditional Anishinaabe floral patterns, woodland imagery, and earth-toned color palettes pay homage to the tribe's artistic traditions while creating contemporary pieces for modern homes.

Each vase incorporates elements inspired by Michigan's natural landscapes - the flowing rivers, forest textures, and seasonal changes that have shaped the Saginaw people's way of life for generations.
