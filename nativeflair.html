The Artist's Story

As a visually impaired artist with deep roots in the Saginaw Chippewa community, I find beauty in transforming ordinary vases into extraordinary works of art. Each piece is painted with careful attention to detail, creating intricate interior designs that tell stories of heritage, nature, and soul.

My work is inspired by traditional Anishinaabe patterns and the natural beauty of Michigan's landscapes. Despite visual challenges, I've developed techniques that allow me to create these detailed interior paintings, each one a unique expression of cultural pride and artistic passion.

Every vase is a journey - from the initial inspiration to the final brushstroke. I believe that art should be accessible to everyone, and my work reflects this philosophy through designs that can be appreciated from multiple perspectives.
