🧠 Consent Basics

Consent isn’t just sexy—it’s necessary. Especially in nightlife spaces where people are vulnerable, intoxicated, overstimulated, or all of the above.

✅ Consent is:

  • Freely given – Not coerced, pressured, or guilt-tripped.
  • Reversible – You can say yes, then change your mind at any time.
  • Informed – Everyone knows exactly what’s going on.
  • Enthusiastic – If it’s not a hell yes, it’s a no.
  • Specific – Saying yes to one thing doesn’t mean yes to everything.

(That spells FRIES if you need to remember it.)

🚫 Not consent:

  • “They didn’t say no.”
  • “They were into it earlier.”
  • “They didn’t fight me.”
  • “They were drunk/high.”
  • “They didn’t respond.”

🎶 In rave spaces:

  • Loud music + substances + vibes ≠ implied consent.
  • Don’t touch people without asking—not even to dance, grab hands, or hug.
  • Don’t assume someone’s outfit, flirting, or dancing means anything more than what it is.
  • People who are rolling, drunk, tripping, or otherwise altered can’t legally or ethically give consent.

⚠️ Quick check-in phrases:

  • “Are you good with this?”
  • “Want me to keep going?”
  • “Let me know if you want to stop.”

Keep it clear, casual, and ongoing. Consent isn’t one question—it’s a whole-ass conversation.

🏳️‍🌈 Respect all identities.

Don’t misgender people. Don’t make assumptions. Don’t use someone's gender or sexuality as an excuse to be invasive or weird. Transphobia, homophobia, and racism = not welcome here.

🔥 What to do if someone crosses a line:

  • If it’s happening now, intervene safely or get help.
  • Support the person—not the abuser, not the rumors.
  • Believe survivors. Listen first. Don’t center yourself.
  • Consent violations aren’t just private drama—they’re community safety issues.
