Consent is an Ongoing Process

True consent culture extends far beyond getting a "yes" or respecting a "no." It's about creating environments where everyone feels empowered to communicate their boundaries and have them respected throughout every interaction.

Continuous Check-ins

Consent isn't a one-time question. Check in regularly with your partners, especially as situations evolve or intensify.

Non-Verbal Cues Matter

Pay attention to body language. Stiffening, pulling away, or avoiding eye contact may indicate discomfort even when words don't.

Enthusiastic Participation

Look for active participation rather than passive acceptance. The absence of a "no" doesn't equal the presence of a "yes."

Changing Minds is Normal

People can withdraw consent at any time, for any reason. Respecting this is fundamental to consent culture.

Remember: Consent isn't just about avoiding violation‚Äîit's about actively creating safety and respect.

Quick Tips for Consent Culture

Communicate Clearly

Use direct language and check for understanding. Don't rely on assumptions or hints.

Respect Boundaries

When someone sets a boundary, honor it immediately without question or negotiation.

Be an Active Bystander

If you see something that doesn't look right, check in with the person who might need support.

Check Your Intentions

Ask yourself why you want to interact with someone. Is it for mutual connection or your own gratification?
