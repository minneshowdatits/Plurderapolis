Why it matters: Most rave drug info isn't made with AFAB folks in mind

How Birth Control and Hormones Affect Your Roll (and More)

Hormones, cycles, and birth control can change how substances hit, and people deserve to know that.

Estrogen + Stimulants

  • Higher estrogen levels (like mid-cycle or on some birth controls) can intensify effects of MDMA and other stimulants.
  • This can increase risk of overheating, serotonin syndrome, or comedown crash.

Hormones + Psychedelics

  • Hormonal shifts can change how you experience shrooms, LSD, or DMT.
  • Some users report stronger visuals or more emotional depth around ovulation or during PMS.

Birth Control + Liver Enzymes

  • Some substances (like speed or certain RCs) compete for liver enzymes.
  • Hormonal BC can slow or intensify metabolization‚Äîmeaning unpredictable roll length or strength.

No Research, No Protection

  • Clinical drug trials often exclude AFAB folks or don't study interactions with HRT/BC.
  • That means DIY harm reduction matters more than ever.

What You Can Do

  • Track your cycle alongside substance use
  • Use lower doses at high-estrogen times
  • Talk openly about what you're taking
  • Share your experience to help others

üß† The Reality of Substance Interactions With Mental Health Meds

Mixing Party Drugs & Mental Health Meds? What You Really Need to Know

SSRIs (like Lexapro, Zoloft, Prozac) + Stimulants

  • Can increase risk of serotonin syndrome ‚Äî symptoms include agitation, rapid heart rate, sweating, confusion, and in severe cases, seizures or death.
  • Stimulants (cocaine, MDMA) already boost serotonin‚Äîmixing can push levels dangerously high.
  • Always watch for sudden changes in mood, heart rate, or temperature.

Benzodiazepines (Xanax, Valium) + Alcohol + Other Depressants

  • Combining benzos with alcohol or opioids causes compounded sedation and respiratory depression.
  • This combo can lead to unconsciousness or fatal overdose.
  • Narcan only reverses opioid overdose‚Äînot benzo or alcohol sedation.

Mood Stabilizers & Antipsychotics (Lithium, Abilify, Seroquel) + Drugs

  • Many of these meds alter metabolism of party drugs, making effects unpredictable.
  • Mixing with stimulants can cause heart issues or severe anxiety.
  • Combining with depressants may worsen sedation or lower blood pressure dangerously.

Other Risks to Know:

  • Psychiatric meds can blunt or intensify drug effects‚Äîleading to accidental overdose or harmful behaviors.
  • Some meds affect the liver's ability to process substances, raising toxicity.
  • Discontinuing or missing psychiatric meds while partying risks relapse or severe mental health symptoms.

Harm Reduction Tips:

  • Always talk with your prescriber about your drug use‚Äîno shame, just facts.
  • Avoid mixing stimulants and SSRIs without medical advice.
  • Carry naloxone if you use opioids or benzos with other depressants.
  • Have a trusted sober friend aware of your meds and signs of overdose or distress.
  • Test your substances whenever possible.

Mental health meds save lives

Treat them like the serious business they are. In Minnesota's rave scene, misinformation kills. Stay informed, stay cautious, and protect yourself and your crew.
