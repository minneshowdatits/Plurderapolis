Minnesota-Specific Resources

Local harm reduction services operating under Minnesota statutes and regulations

Narcan/Naloxone Access

MN Department of Health-approved distribution sites. Available without prescription per MN Statute 151.37.

MN Good Samaritan Law

MN Statute 604A.04 provides limited immunity for those seeking medical assistance for overdose.

Syringe Services

MN Statute 151.40 authorizes syringe service programs for harm reduction.

Crisis Response

MN Crisis Text Line: Text "MN" to 741741. Mobile crisis teams available statewide.

üö® Emergency Assistance

If someone is unresponsive:

  1. Check for responsiveness - tap shoulder and shout
  2. Call 911 immediately
  3. Check breathing - look for chest movement
  4. Administer Narcan if available and trained
  5. Perform rescue breathing if not breathing
  6. Place in recovery position if breathing

Minnesota Good Samaritan Law

Under MN Statute 604A.04, a person acting in good faith who seeks medical assistance for another person who is experiencing a drug-related overdose is immune from criminal prosecution for possession of a controlled substance.
