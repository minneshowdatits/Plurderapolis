üö® Emergency Assistance

If someone is unresponsive:

  1. Call 911 immediately - You are protected by Minnesota's Good Samaritan Law
  2. Check for breathing - Look for chest movement, listen for breaths
  3. Administer naloxone if available (even if unsure about opioids)
  4. Perform rescue breathing if not breathing (1 breath every 5 seconds)
  5. Stay with the person until help arrives
  6. Place in recovery position if breathing

Harm Reduction Principles

  • Test substances before use (fentanyl test strips available in MN)
  • Start with a small amount (low and slow)
  • Never use alone
  • Have naloxone available
  • Know the signs of overdose
  • Stay hydrated but don't overhydrate
  • Take regular breaks
