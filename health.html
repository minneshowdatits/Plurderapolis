Raver Survival Guide 🌈✨

  • Sip, don’t chug. Electrolytes > plain water.
  • Bring electrolyte packets (cheap + tiny).
  • Signs of dehydration: headache, dizziness, dry mouth, hot skin.
  • More on hydration + overheating →
  • Eat something carby + salty before you head in.
  • Don’t experiment with brand-new drugs or combos at the show.
  • Dress for temp swings (sweaty inside, cold outside).
  • Have your “home safe” plan figured out.
  • Check polydrug combos before you go →
  • Pace yourself: You don’t need to rail every 15 mins.
  • Rotate: dance, chill, hydrate, breathe.
  • Snorting? Use your own straw/spoon. Don’t share. Rinse between bumps.
  • Take breaks in fresh air.
  • Keep track of your crew (and their mental state, too).
  • See safe snorting tips →
  • Rehydrate with electrolytes, not just water.
  • Eat something easy on the stomach (bananas, rice, broth).
  • Sleep in a dark, cool room.
  • Vitamins: magnesium + vitamin C help recovery.
  • Recovery + comedown tips →
  • Signs someone’s not okay: unresponsive, seizing, turning blue, can’t stay conscious.
  • Call 911 — Good Samaritan laws protect you.
  • Use the Emergency Panic Button if you’re unsure what to do.
  • Carry naloxone & test strips.
  • Full overdose & emergency guide →
  • Space your rolls: serotonin isn’t infinite.
  • Know your patterns — partying every weekend hits harder than you think.
  • Therapy, rest, and hobbies outside the rave keep you balanced.
  • Mental health resources →
  • Baby Vicks: For rails, stuffy noses, or quick airflow relief.
  • Gum & lollipops: Jaw clench + dry mouth saver.
  • Bandana: Dust, sweat wipe, or quick mask.
  • Portable charger: Lifeline for rideshare, group check-ins, emergencies.
  • AirTag / location sharing: Keep your bag and friends locatable.
  • Festival body survival: Sunscreen, extra socks, poncho, foot care—Minnesota weather is unpredictable.
  • Environmental care: Don’t litter. Dispose of sharps/test kits properly.
  • Digital harm reduction: Don’t post people railing or blacked out; respect privacy.
  • Consent culture: PLUR isn’t just bracelets—respect, community care, watch out for the vulnerable.

🆘 Crisis Support Tools

Click a resource to learn when and how to use it.

🌀 Bad Trip Mode (PLURderapolis)

Bad Trip Mode is a page with grounding tips, calm visuals, and audio loops designed to help someone through a difficult psychedelic experience. Useful mid-trip when someone’s spiraling or overwhelmed.

💉 MN Resources (PLURderapolis)

Local MN Resources helps you find Narcan, test strips, queer-friendly clinics, recovery groups, and more — all based in Minnesota. Use this if you or a friend need real-life support, fast.

📚 DanceSafe

DanceSafe is a harm reduction organization with detailed drug fact sheets and testing kit info. Use it before you take anything to check dosages, risks, and effects.

👂 TripSit

TripSit offers a live anonymous chat where volunteers will stay with you during a bad trip or tough combo. Use it when you're alone and scared and need calm, non-judgy backup.

🔥 Fireside Project

Fireside Project is a phone/text line for psychedelic support. Great for integration or talking someone down gently. Not just during the trip — also after.

📞 Never Use Alone

Never Use Alone is a service where you can call or text before you use opioids. They’ll stay on the line and call help if you stop responding. Crucial if you're using alone or worried about fentanyl.
