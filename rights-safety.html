Rights While Peacefully Protesting

  • You have the right to peaceably assemble in public spaces
  • You can record law enforcement activity as long as you don't physically obstruct them
  • You can refuse consent to searches (vehicle, bag, person)
  • If ordered to disperse lawfully, comply to reduce arrest risk
If Arrested: Stay calm & silent. Clearly state: "I am exercising my right to remain silent. I want a lawyer." Do not sign anything without legal counsel.

üì± Digital Safety

Protect your privacy during events and encounters.
  • Use Strong Passcode
    Use passcode instead of biometrics (Face ID/Touch ID) before sensitive situations.
  • Manage Location Services
    Turn off location sharing for social media and sensitive apps.
  • Know Your Rights
    You generally do not have to unlock your phone for law enforcement without a warrant.
  • Secure Communications
    Consider using encrypted messaging apps like Signal for sensitive communications.
    Download Signal

Digital & Privacy Safety Tips

  • Your phone and online presence are tools ‚Äî but they can expose you or others if misused
  • Be cautious posting real-time locations of people in potentially volatile areas
  • Store important legal numbers in a secure place that's quick to access
  • Avoid sharing personal information publicly when seeking help
Remember: Store important legal numbers in a secure note app or write them on your arm with permanent marker.

‚ö†Ô∏è Chemical Agent Safety

First aid for exposure to pepper spray, tear gas, or chemical irritants.
  • Exposure to Chemical Agents & First Aid
    Risks like pepper spray, tear gas, or chemical irritants sometimes occur near large law enforcement engagements.
  • Red Cross First Aid Guide
    Official first aid instructions for chemical exposure and other emergencies.
    View Guide

First Aid Steps

  • Do not rub your eyes if exposed ‚Äî blinking repeatedly can help flush irritants
  • Rinse eyes and skin with cool water or saline (milk is not a recommended treatment)
  • Remove contaminated clothing and seek fresh air immediately
  • People with asthma or breathing issues should seek medical attention promptly
  • Move uphill and upwind if possible, as chemical agents tend to settle in low areas
Important: This guidance is general emergency first aid; consult medical professionals whenever possible for proper treatment.

üÜò Emergency Assistance

If someone is unresponsive:

  1. Check for responsiveness - tap shoulder and shout
  2. Call 911 immediately
  3. Check breathing - look for chest movement
  4. Administer Narcan if available and trained
  5. Perform rescue breathing if not breathing
  6. Place in recovery position if breathing

If stopped by police or ICE:

  • Stay calm and keep your hands visible
  • You have the right to remain silent
  • Ask "Am I free to leave?"
  • Do not consent to searches
  • Ask for a lawyer immediately if detained
