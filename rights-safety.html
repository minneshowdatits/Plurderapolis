Rights While Peacefully Protesting

  • You have the right to peaceably assemble in public spaces
  • You can record law enforcement activity as long as you don't physically obstruct them
  • You can refuse consent to searches (vehicle, bag, person)
  • If ordered to disperse lawfully, comply to reduce arrest risk

If Arrested

Stay calm & silent. Clearly state: "I am exercising my right to remain silent. I want a lawyer." Do not sign anything without legal counsel.

Digital Safety & Privacy

▼

Protect your privacy and digital security during events, protests, and encounters with law enforcement.

  • Use Strong Passcode

    Use passcode instead of biometrics (Face ID/Touch ID) before sensitive situations. Law enforcement can compel biometric unlock but not passcodes in most cases.

  • Manage Location Services

    Turn off location sharing for social media and sensitive apps. Review app permissions regularly.

  • Secure Communications

    Consider using encrypted messaging apps like Signal for sensitive communications.

    Download Signal

Digital Safety Tips

  • Your phone and online presence are tools — but they can expose you or others if misused
  • Be cautious posting real-time locations of people in potentially volatile areas
  • Store important legal numbers in a secure place that's quick to access
  • Avoid sharing personal information publicly when seeking help

Emergency Assistance

If someone is unresponsive:

  1. Check for responsiveness - tap shoulder and shout
  2. Call 911 immediately
  3. Check breathing - look for chest movement
  4. Administer Narcan if available and trained
  5. Perform rescue breathing if not breathing
  6. Place in recovery position if breathing

If stopped by police or ICE:

  • Stay calm and keep your hands visible
  • You have the right to remain silent
  • Ask "Am I free to leave?"
  • Do not consent to searches
  • Ask for a lawyer immediately if detained
