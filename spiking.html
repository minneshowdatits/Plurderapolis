What is Drink Spiking?

▼

Drink spiking occurs when someone adds alcohol, drugs, or other substances to another person's drink without their knowledge or consent. These substances are often colorless, odorless, and tasteless, making them difficult to detect. In some cases, substances may even be applied to the outside of cans or bottles.

Drink spiking is a serious crime that can lead to:

  • Sexual assault or rape - Perpetrators often use spiking to incapacitate victims
  • Robbery or theft - Taking advantage of impaired consciousness
  • Physical harm or injury - Due to loss of coordination and consciousness
  • Severe health consequences - Including overdose, allergic reactions, or long-term effects

Trust Your Instincts

You may never get a clear warning sign until it's too late. If something feels off about your drink, your environment, or how you're feeling, trust your gut immediately. Your safety is more important than being polite or not wanting to make a scene.

Signs Your Drink May Have Been Tampered With

▼

Being able to recognize the warning signs of drink tampering can help you stay safe. Look for these indicators:

  • Change in taste - Bitter, salty, or metallic taste that wasn't there before
  • Cloudy or foggy appearance - Especially in drinks that should be clear
  • Excessive or unusual bubbles - In non-carbonated beverages
  • Ice behaving oddly - Sinking when it should float, or discoloration
  • Unexpected smell changes - Even subtle odor differences
  • Film or residue - On the surface of the drink or around the rim

Important Safety Note

Many date rape drugs have no odor, taste, or color. Always trust your instincts over these physical signs. If you feel uneasy about your drink for any reason, stop drinking it immediately and get a new, sealed beverage.

Symptoms of Being Spiked

▼

The effects of drink spiking can vary depending on the substance used, but common symptoms include:

  • Feeling disproportionately drunk - Much more intoxicated than the amount consumed would suggest
  • Sudden drowsiness or fatigue - Overwhelming need to sleep
  • Confusion or disorientation - Not knowing where you are or what's happening
  • Slurred speech - Difficulty forming words clearly
  • Loss of balance or coordination - Difficulty walking or standing
  • Nausea or vomiting - Especially if you haven't consumed much alcohol
  • Visual disturbances - Blurred vision, double vision, or dizziness
  • Memory loss or blackouts - Gaps in memory of recent events
  • Unusual body temperature - Feeling excessively hot or cold
  • Breathing difficulties - Shallow breathing or feeling breathless

Medical Attention is Crucial

If you or someone you know is experiencing these symptoms, seek medical attention immediately. Some substances used in drink spiking can be life-threatening.

Immediate Action Steps

▼

If you suspect you or someone else has been spiked, take these steps immediately:

Step 1: Stop Drinking

Do not take another sip. If possible, cover or set the drink aside to preserve evidence.

Step 2: Get to Safety

Move away from crowds and stay with trusted friends. Avoid being alone.

Step 3: Seek Medical Help

Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room, especially for serious symptoms.

Step 4: Preserve Evidence

Save the drink and container. Keep clothing in a paper bag. Don't shower or wash.

Minnesota Good Samaritan Law Protection

You're protected under Minnesota's Good Samaritan Law (MN Stat § 604A.05). If someone needs help, call 911. You're doing the right thing — and you're legally safe from prosecution for minor drug or alcohol violations when seeking medical assistance.

Prevention & Safety Tips

▼

Protect yourself and your friends with these evidence-based prevention strategies:

  • Never leave your drink unattended - If you do, discard it immediately
  • Watch your drink being prepared - Accept only drinks you see being opened or poured
  • Use drink covers or lids - Specialized covers can prevent tampering
  • Practice the buddy system - Go out in groups and watch out for each other
  • Designate a drink watcher - When someone steps away, have a friend watch their drink
  • Be cautious with strangers - Avoid accepting drinks from people you don't know well
  • Speak up if you suspect something - Notify venue staff immediately
  • Stick to bottled drinks with secure lids - Harder to tamper with than open cups

Community Protection Strategies

Look out for others: If you see someone who appears overly intoxicated or in distress, check on them. Offer to call a friend or taxi. Venue staff training: Ask your favorite venues about their staff training on drink spiking prevention and response.
